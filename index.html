The Stats

According to the Merriam-Webster dictionary, social media is defined as forms of electronic communication (such as websites for social networking and microblogging) through which users create online communities to share information, ideas, personal messages, and other content (such as videos). Some of the more popular social networking sites include Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and YouTube. According to social media management resource Hootsuite, Facebook has about 74% of its active users on the site everyday. Twitter has about 46%. Instagram has 60%. And YouTube has about 45%. The average user spends about an hour each day on social media. Considering the fact that the amount of users on these platforms are excess of 1 billion people, these numbers account for a significant amount of people on social media for a significant amount of time. The Effects

Fear of Missing Out

Young adults may experience fear of missing out when away from their social media because that is likely their main source for daily information about what's going on in the world. Gif source: MakeAGif.com. According to a BBC observation, students found that although they missed out on checking their feeds, they found that they had a lot more time to focus on other things. Gif by: Cryptoman.

Anxiety

Young adults may experience anxiety from social media addiction. The constant stress of being overwhelmed with information and comparing oneself to another can take a toll on mental health. According to the Anxiety and Depression Association of America, this anxiety can also lead to loss of interest in other activities and severe nervousness when you are not able to check your notifications. Gif source: Giphy.com. Gif by: Zetrystan.

Distraction

Acute distraction is also a consequence of being addicted to social media. Many college-aged students can relate to losing countless hours of sleep and productivity just from being so caught up in social media. According to TeamLease World of Work Report, 13% of total productivity is lost due to social media indulgence alone. The distraction can also play out in other aspects of an addict's life.
